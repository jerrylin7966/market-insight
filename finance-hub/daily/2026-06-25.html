What's Moving Markets Today

Thursday, June 25, 2026
By MarketPhase Research
Market Summary
Equity futures and major indices are rising ahead of today's crucial PCE inflation report, with Micron Technology's stellar earnings beat providing crucial momentum to a tech sector that had been under siege. The rally reflects a pivotal market narrative shift: inflation data could validate the case for Fed rate cuts even as Kevin Warsh's potential appointment signals a more hawkish policy stance ahead. Investors are cautiously optimistic that corporate earnings strength—particularly in semiconductors and AI—can overcome macro headwinds, but today's economic data will determine whether this rebound has legs.

Key Stories

Micron's 12% Surge Signals Tech Recovery Amid AI Chip Demand

Micron's earnings beat sent semiconductor and related tech stocks soaring, including SandDisk, validating the thesis that AI-driven demand for memory and storage chips remains robust despite recent market pessimism. This is significant because the semiconductor sector had become a barometer for both AI sentiment and macroeconomic health; a earnings miss would have suggested weakening demand. Investors should monitor whether this strength extends to other chipmakers like Intel and Qualcomm, which both have analyst calls out today and face their own credibility tests.

PCE Inflation Report Looms as Market's Critical Crossroads Today

The Personal Consumption Expenditures report—the Fed's preferred inflation gauge—is dropping today and carries enormous weight for interest rate expectations. A cooler PCE could validate the dovish narrative that inflation is finally under control, supporting equity valuations; a hotter print would reinforce hawkish rate-hike expectations and likely trigger a sharp selloff. This is why gold has already started pricing in rate-hike risk, falling below $4,000 on speculation that Kevin Warsh will take a harder line on inflation than current Fed leadership.

Gold Signals Fed Policy Anxiety as Warsh Appointment Shifts Rate Expectations

Gold's decline below $4,000—the lowest opening since December 2025—reflects market concerns that a Warsh-led Fed would prioritize fighting inflation over supporting growth, potentially delaying or preventing rate cuts that equities have been pricing in. This is a critical tell: precious metals are usually contrarian indicators, and their weakness suggests investors are backing away from inflation-hedge positioning in favor of a disinflationary narrative. Watch whether equity markets can hold gains if today's PCE data reinforces these hawkish rate-hike expectations.

Quantum Computing and Energy Stocks Find Tailwinds in Structural Themes

Quantinuum surged 13.5% on news that the U.S. wants to accelerate quantum computing development, while energy plays like NatPower and Tesla inked a major 25 GWh European battery storage deal and Constellation Energy outperformed on analyst support. These gains highlight a bifurcated market: niche, high-growth secular themes (quantum, AI infrastructure, clean energy) are finding buyers even as broad-based tech has stumbled, suggesting sophisticated capital is rotating into genuine innovation versus crowded mega-cap AI bets.

Biotech M&A Narrative Gains Traction with Multiple Upgrades and Regulatory Wins

Iovance jumped 9.4% on a regulatory approval while Viking Therapeutics surged on Medicare implications, and Street analysts are openly discussing biotech as an M&A target category. This suggests large pharma companies may be deploying capital into smaller biotech names as a more efficient path to innovation than internal R&D, a significant structural shift for the sector. Investors should note this as a potential valuation floor for quality biotech—consolidation premiums could support beaten-down stocks in this space.

Enterprise Software Under Pressure; SAP Stumbles on Goldman Margin Concerns

SAP fell after Goldman Sachs cut margin forecasts, extending weakness in the software sector where Accenture (down 50% YTD) and Intuit (down 57% YTD) are trading at distressed levels. These declines suggest investors are repricing enterprise software for lower growth and margin compression, likely driven by AI automation fears (fewer consulting hours needed) and budget-tightening from corporate clients. This is a sector to avoid until we see evidence of stabilization in guidance and margin trends.

UK Industrial Activity Supports Global Trade Narrative; Tesla Battery Deal Highlights Infrastructure Race

UK car production rising and the Tesla-NatPower battery storage deal illustrate that industrial activity remains solid at the margins, even as macro concerns swirl. This supports the case that a hard landing is not imminent, though growth is clearly moderating. The battery storage deal, in particular, signals that energy infrastructure investment—driven by AI data center demand and energy transition—could be a durable growth engine regardless of whether broader equities struggle.

Sectors in Focus

Technology is the day's dominant narrative, splitting into winners and losers: semiconductors and AI infrastructure (Micron, quantum, battery storage) are rallying hard, while enterprise software (SAP, Accenture, Intuit) is collapsing under margin-compression fears. Healthcare and biotech are finding support from M&A speculation and regulatory wins, providing defensive positioning. Energy stocks are mixed but supported by structural tailwinds in battery storage and infrastructure investment. The real story is that the 'AI' trade has become highly fragmented—picking specific infrastructure winners is being rewarded while broad-based software and services weakness is being punished.

Macro Note

Today's PCE inflation report is the hinge pin for near-term market direction, with a cooler print potentially validating rate-cut expectations priced into equities, while a hotter print would shift power to the hawkish Warsh narrative and support the recent gold selloff that signals rate-hike risk. The market is essentially trading two competing Fed scenarios: the dovish case (inflation conquered, cuts coming) versus the hawkish case (inflation stickier than hoped, rates stay higher for longer). Earnings resilience in semiconductors and other pockets of strength suggest the economy is not in freefall, but growth is clearly decelerating, leaving the Fed in a delicate position that today's data will help clarify.

What This Means For You

Individual investors should treat today as a true inflection point: if PCE comes in cool, the recent tech recovery could accelerate and risk assets broadly resume momentum; if it prints hot, expect a rotation out of growth stocks and into defensive positioning. The bifurcation between AI infrastructure winners (semiconductors, quantum, energy storage) and enterprise software losers is telling you that market leadership is shifting from mega-cap software to more specialized, capital-intensive AI suppliers—this is a critical dynamic for portfolio positioning. Avoid broad-based tech exposure until we see signs of stabilization in enterprise software margins, but consider accumulating quality semiconductor and quantum plays on any PCE-driven weakness. Watch the Fed's reaction to today's data closely: if Warsh's hawkish stance gains credibility, defensive sectors and commodities could outperform for months, a major shift from recent sentiment.

MarketPhase Take

We are witnessing a classic earnings-driven recovery within a macro crisis narrative, and today's inflation data will determine whether this relief rally is durable or a bear-market bounce. The market's simultaneous weakness in enterprise software and strength in semiconductor/quantum infrastructure reveals that investors are fundamentally repricing what 'AI winning' actually means—it's not a software revolution, it's a hardware/infrastructure arms race where capital intensity and supply constraints favor specific winners. The Warsh Fed pivot is real and important: if confirmed, it extends the period of higher-for-longer rates and deflates the multiple expansion thesis that equities have been counting on. We believe today's PCE report will show stubborn inflation, validating Warsh's hawkish instincts and triggering a 2-3% index pullback before we stabilize.

Market Outlook

Today's PCE inflation report (due this afternoon) is the critical near-term catalyst that will set the tone for the rest of June and beyond. Tomorrow's jobs report next week will provide the secondary confirmation of labor market softness, and if both data points show cooling, the Fed rate-cut narrative could accelerate into Q3. Watch Fed speakers and Warsh commentary closely over the next 48 hours for clues on whether a rate-cut window is truly opening or if hawkish tightening bias will persist; this will determine whether today's tech bounce has staying power or represents a bear-market trap.
