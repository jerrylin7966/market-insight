What's Moving Markets Today

Thursday, July 9, 2026
By MarketPhase Research
Market Summary
U.S. equities extended gains today as investors balanced geopolitical tensions against compelling valuations in mega-cap technology stocks, particularly AI-focused names trading at their cheapest levels in over a decade. The market's resilience reflects a classic risk-on setup: despite U.S.-Iran friction and crypto weakness, the fundamental case for AI infrastructure investment remains intact, with Micron, Meta, and Alphabet all capturing investor attention. However, the breadth of the rally masks important weakness in software stocks and mid-cap names, signaling that growth enthusiasm remains narrowly concentrated. Futures suggested continued upside, but the persistence of geopolitical headlines suggests any pop could face selling pressure.

Key Stories

AT&T Leaves Rivals Flat-Footed as Bankrupt Carrier Folds

A major telecom competitor exiting the market through bankruptcy represents a significant consolidation event that reshuffles competitive dynamics for AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile. This outcome suggests that smaller carriers cannot compete at scale in the 5G era—a validation of the "network oligopoly" thesis that has benefited the Big Three. Investors should monitor whether this triggers a new wave of M&A activity or allows incumbents to raise pricing power without competitive pressure, a potential tailwind for telecom dividend stocks.

Magnificent 7 Trading at Cheapest Valuation in 10+ Years Amid AI Rally

The narrative of expensive mega-cap tech stocks has been materially challenged by today's revelation that valuations have compressed to historically attractive levels despite the AI boom. This suggests the market has repriced expectations downward on these names—meaning either growth expectations have fallen or the market was overvaluing them previously. For value-oriented investors, this creates a genuine entry point, but it also signals that AI enthusiasm may be tempering optimism about near-term returns, a critical inflection point to monitor.

Meta Commits $9 Billion to Canadian AI Data Center; Micron Pledges $250 Billion through 2035

Today's capex commitments from two critical AI infrastructure players confirm that the investment cycle in AI chips and computing capacity is not a temporary spike but a sustained, multi-year commitment. Meta's international expansion of data center footprint also diversifies geopolitical risk and positions the company to capture non-U.S. AI adoption. These moves are bullish for semiconductor suppliers like Micron and NVIDIA but also raise questions about ROI—management is betting heavily that AI monetization will justify these outlays, a bet that hasn't fully played out yet.

K-Shaped Spending: Top 10% Spends Nearly as Much on Nonessentials as Bottom 70% Combined

This data point cuts to the heart of today's consumer-driven market: wealth concentration is accelerating, and luxury/discretionary spending is being driven by the affluent while lower-income cohorts pull back. This explains the paradox of resilient stock markets amid consumer spending concerns—investors with portfolio exposure to premium brands and services benefit disproportionately. For equity investors, this is a structural headwind for mass-market retailers and consumer staples names, while creating tailwinds for luxury goods, high-end services, and platforms catering to affluent consumers.

Salesforce Downgraded on Agentforce Product Concerns; ServiceNow Sold Off Despite Solid Results

Enterprise software stocks faced meaningful pressure today despite operational performance, signaling investor skepticism about the AI-as-a-service narrative that has dominated the narrative for months. The specific concern about Salesforce's Agentforce suggests that AI implementation in business workflows remains unproven—execution risk is real. This is a critical warning sign for the software sector broadly: valuations assumed aggressive AI monetization, but today's weakness suggests the market is repricing that assumption downward, creating a potential sector-wide reset.

SpaceX Spending Spree Could Threaten AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile

Elon Musk's aggressive expansion of Starlink satellite internet services is now being viewed as a competitive threat to legacy telecom infrastructure and pricing power. As SpaceX scales deployment and coverage, it could cannibalize revenue from traditional ISPs and undermine the pricing discipline telecom operators have enjoyed. This is a longer-term structural risk for the telecom sector, suggesting that today's AT&T bankruptcy consolidation may not be the end of disruption—it may be the beginning.

Bitcoin and Ethereum Prices Sliding on Geopolitical Tensions

Cryptocurrency weakness despite equity strength reveals a bifurcation in risk-on assets: traditional equities are holding up on AI fundamentals, while cryptos are selling off as geopolitical fears drive flight-to-safety moves. This suggests that the U.S.-Iran tensions are being priced as a tail risk that makes alternative assets less attractive, even as equity investors rationalize fundamental support for AI and tech stocks. Monitor crypto for capitulation signals—if digital assets stabilize while equities hold, it could confirm that geopolitical fears are contained.

Sectors in Focus

Technology and semiconductors led today's rally, with Micron, Meta, and Alphabet capturing investor attention as AI infrastructure and cloud spending continue to drive earnings growth. However, the weakness in enterprise software—particularly Salesforce and ServiceNow despite solid results—suggests that the software sector is experiencing valuation compression as investors question AI monetization assumptions. Telecommunications gained from AT&T's competitive consolidation win, but face longer-term disruption risk from SpaceX/Starlink expansion. Consumer discretionary weakness tied to K-shaped spending patterns suggests that traditional retail and mass-market consumer names will lag, while luxury and affluent-focused segments continue to outperform.

Macro Note

Geopolitical tensions between the U.S. and Iran are creating a low-level risk-off backdrop, evident in cryptocurrency weakness and defensive positioning, yet equity markets are proving resilient on the strength of AI fundamentals and tight labor markets. Interest rates remain the critical variable not visible in today's headlines—the Fed's policy path will determine whether current equity valuations are sustainable or represent a valuation trap. Inflation data and Fed communications remain the macro wildcard, with any hawkish surprise likely to trigger a sharp repricing in growth stocks currently benefiting from the AI narrative. Corporate capex commitments suggest management confidence in long-term growth, but also represent a bet on sustained high returns on invested capital that hasn't yet materialized.

What This Means For You

Today's market action presents three critical insights for long-term investors. First, the compression of Magnificent 7 valuations to decade-low levels creates a genuine opportunity to establish or add positions in quality AI beneficiaries, but only for investors with conviction that AI monetization will drive 10+ year returns—this is not a "value trap" safety signal, but rather a reset on growth expectations. Second, the weakness in enterprise software despite solid results is a critical red flag: if AI-as-a-service cannot command premium valuations even with execution, the entire software sector may be repricing downward, suggesting selective positioning over broad sector exposure. Third, monitor the K-shaped spending pattern closely—if affluent consumer weakness accelerates, it would undermine the resilience narrative and signal that equity gains are concentrated in too narrow a set of beneficiaries (semiconductors, cloud, AI infrastructure) to sustain a broad market advance. Watch for next week's earnings reports and any guidance revisions that would confirm or contradict today's software-sector weakness.

MarketPhase Take

We are in the mature phase of a growth-driven bull market where valuations have reset but momentum remains intact—this is the moment when selectivity becomes brutal and breadth matters far more than headlines. The AT&T bankruptcy and Salesforce downgrade are not isolated events; they signal that market leadership is narrowing precisely when that concentration becomes most dangerous. The AI rally is real, but it is being priced into fewer names at lower multiples, creating an efficiency problem: if all the structural growth is baked into semiconductors and cloud infrastructure, what pulls the rest of the market higher? The geopolitical backdrop provides convenient cover for selective profit-taking, but the real risk is that equity markets have already repriced too far on AI assumptions and are vulnerable to any disappointment in monetization or returns on the massive capex cycle we're seeing. We recommend using strength in mega-cap tech to rebalance into beaten-down software and industrials that offer asymmetric upside if the broadening thesis plays out.

Market Outlook

Key events over the coming days include any developments in U.S.-Iran tensions (could trigger sharp equity selloff), next week's retail earnings reports (critical for validating K-shaped spending thesis), and any Fed commentary that could shift rate expectations. Treasury yield movements will be critical—if yields compress on geopolitical fears, growth stocks face headwinds; if yields rise on inflation concerns, the AI capex thesis becomes more at risk. Monitor crypto for stabilization as a signal that geopolitical fears are contained; continued weakness would confirm that risk-off sentiment is broader than headlines suggest.
